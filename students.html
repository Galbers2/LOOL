Steps Students Can Take to Start a Food Recovery Program

  1. Join the school green team or environmental club. Share your idea and be prepared to persevere and work.
  2. Talk to the principal and gain their support, along with building services. If there is no green team, recruit friends and start one.
  3. Conduct a waste sort or observe what is in the trash. Does your school recycle? Is it contaminated? Does it have a share table?
  4. Reach out to the local Rotary Club or an Edward Jones consultant for funding. Our website can help you find them.
  5. Once you have funding for a refrigerator, select a start date for your program launch.
  6. Recruit green team members to monitor waste stations, coach peers, and raise awareness about food waste reduction.
  7. You may face resistance-do not take no for an answer. Your voice matters!

If you're a high school student, join the green team and look for an Interact Club to help with funding. Ask about earning Service Learning Hours or internships. You can even volunteer at a local elementary school to support their refrigerator program.

Start with food recovery, then expand into recycling, plastic film collection with NexTrex, and composting programs.

Remember: As a student, your voice is powerful. Everyone working at the school is there to support you. Be empowered. Be heard. You have the power to make change!
