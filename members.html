Community Members

Your support can drive change in your local schools. Follow these steps to get involved:

  1. Identify a local school cluster. Elementary schools produce the most waste; high school and middle school students can serve as mentors and earn service hours or internships.
  2. Visit during lunch. Does the school have a share table or recycling program? Are perishables like milk kept chilled?
  3. Look for a green team or environmental club. If none exists, ask the principal if a staff member might be interested in starting one.
  4. Connect with building services. Let them know students will monitor the fridgeâ€”reducing their workload.
  5. Set up a food collection system for Friday pickups to a food bank or church. Fridges must be cleaned weekly.
  6. Roll out the program with posters and announcements. Make sure students know what food can be recovered.
  7. Volunteer at the start and periodically check in with the green team to ensure things run smoothly.
  8. Help transport food to donation sites or coordinate a Friday pickup system.
  9. Connect with local Rotary Clubs and Edward Jones consultants. We can help you make those connections!
