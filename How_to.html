1. Recover Uneaten Food


Food donation is indemnified by the Emerson Good Samaritan Food Recovery Act protection entities from liability, however the safe handling of perishable food is essential. This option requires installation of a mini refrigerator inside cafeterias where students can place unwanted milks, yogurt and other perishable food items. Schools can solicit fundsto pay for the refrigerator and a student led green team should monitor and clean refrigerators and arrange for food runners to deliver to a local food bank weekly.


2. Decontaminate Recycling

Many schools do not recycle and deposit recyclable materials in the trash. It is cheaper to landfill than to recycle and most recycling containers are badly contaminated. School green team leaders and students have the opportunity to teach schools which items can be recycled and ensure contamination is eliminated. Student green teams can volunteer to collect recycle containers from cafeterias as well as classrooms, extract contaminated materials and ensure the material is correctly place in the recycle dumpsters.


3. Extract Liquids

If liquids are left in containers those containers are no longer recyclable. Providing a 5 gallon bucket with a bucket screen can eliminate as much as 20% of the weight of cafeteria trash. 4. Plastic Film Collection

NexTrex collects plastic films as a building material for Trex plastic decking. Plastic film generated in schools is minimal but if students can collect plastic film from home and bring to school on designated collection days and participate in the NexTrex challenge. Flexible plastic materials can be weighed by students and taken to the local supermarket where the material is taken to the distribution center to be baled and collected by Trex in every community in the US


5. Compost Organic Material

Commercial compost capacity is still limited but increasing exponentially in communities throughout the country, While compost operations on school grounds is ideal it limits organic diversion to fruits and vegetables and done with a high level of expertise. Commercial compost operators can compost anything that can be eaten including meat and dairy and organics comprise 50% of all cafeteria generated waste. Compost haulers collect material weekly in 65 gallon bins that are pest proof and exchanged with each pick up. Yes for hauling can cost as much as $2000 annually but the reduction of 50% of the organics, liquids extraction, proper recycling and food recovery can reduce the number of dumpster pick ups and prove to be cost neutral.
